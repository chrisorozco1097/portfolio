Smart Warehouse Automation

Deployment, programming, and optimization of Laser Guided Vehicles (LGVs) for automated product handling in a smart warehouse environment.

Technologies

LIDAR
TwinCAT
C
HMI

Objective

The primary objective of this project was to implement and optimize the use of Laser Guided Vehicles (LGVs) within a high-capacity smart warehouse, automating the complete flow of materials including product picking, transport, and storage. By integrating cutting-edge automation technology, the project sought to drastically improve operational efficiency, ensure high safety standards, and reduce manual handling, all while maintaining flexibility for different product types and warehouse layouts.

My Role & Techniques

1. Startup & Programming of LGVs

  • Responsible for the programming and configuration of LGVs to ensure accurate product handling, optimal pathfinding, and smooth operation within the warehouse environment.
  • Created customized task workflows within the Warehouse Management System (WMS) for different product categories, allowing automated dispatching and prioritization based on product type, destination, and urgency.

2. Sensor Calibration & Tuning

  • Conducted sensor calibration for multiple detection devices including:
    • Laser scanners for precise navigation and obstacle avoidance.
    • 3D LIDAR for dynamic environment mapping.
    • Load sensors to verify product handling integrity.
  • Performed fine-tuning of proximity sensors, collision avoidance systems, and laser reflectors to guarantee operational accuracy and safety.

3. Real-Time Monitoring & Alarm Systems

  • Developed and tested real-time tracking systems enabling supervisors to monitor the location and status of all LGVs through the central WMS interface.
  • Configured and validated alarm and fault management protocols, ensuring that any navigation error, load misplacement, or safety breach triggered immediate operator notifications with prioritized resolutions.

4. Task Optimization & Traffic Control

  • Programmed intelligent task allocation algorithms based on:
    • Vehicle proximity to task locations.
    • Load type and weight.
    • Priority level of tasks and deadline requirements.
  • Set up collision prevention and traffic management logic ensuring smooth multi-vehicle operations without bottlenecks or route overlaps.

5. Integration & Testing

  • Integrated LGVs with the main production system and WMS, ensuring seamless two-way communication.
  • Managed extensive testing phases including cold starts, emergency stop scenarios, auto-recovery features, and stress tests with full warehouse activity.
Smart Warehouse with operating LGVs.

Results & Impact

  • Achieved full automation of material flows, reducing manual labor intervention by over 80%.
  • Increased storage and retrieval speed by 40%, minimizing downtimes and human errors.
  • Established real-time operational visibility, improving management oversight and responsiveness.
  • Delivered a highly reliable safety environment.
  • Created a scalable solution adaptable to future expansions and new product lines.

Why It Matters

Modern logistics and production environments demand speed, precision, and adaptability. This project contributed directly to achieving those goals by leveraging advanced automation technologies in a practical, high-volume environment. Through real-time tracking, advanced task scheduling, and rigorous safety features, this LGV deployment ensures continuous, uninterrupted operations, providing the company with a competitive edge in material handling efficiency, operational cost reduction, and workplace safety.

This experience also allowed me to develop hands-on expertise in automation systems integration, real-time controls, and smart logistics solutions, skills that are directly transferable to a wide range of industries embracing Industry 4.0 transformation.
