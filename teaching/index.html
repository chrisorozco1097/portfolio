Teaching

Throughout my professional journey, I have consistently pursued opportunities to share knowledge, mentor students, and create engaging learning environments. My teaching experience spans from grassroots educational projects to advanced university-level instruction and international scholarship programs.

I was honored to be selected as a Mathematics Professor for RISE México, an educational program in collaboration with RISE for the World. This prestigious initiative focuses on providing high-quality educational support to talented Mexican students, preparing them for competitive evaluations and college entrance exams.

This fully remote summer program gathered 95 of the most outstanding students from across Mexico, providing a unique opportunity to deliver engaging, innovative mathematics education in a virtual environment.

One of my proudest accomplishments during this program was the creation of "The Math Show" — a fully customized, interactive learning experience combining academic rigor with gamification strategies. The program featured:

  • Video lectures on core math subjects: Number Theory, Calculus, Algebra, and Geometry
  • Weekly Activities with a competitive point system based on both accuracy and speed of submission
  • Live Game Shows inspired by popular TV formats like Family Feud, Wheel of Fortune, and The Price is Right, repurposed to test mathematical knowledge in a fun, collaborative way
  • Weekly leaderboards and performance tracking to motivate consistent participation

The program encouraged creativity, critical thinking, and teamwork in a completely remote setting, delivering impactful education despite geographical barriers.

Previous Next

During my time as a Master’s student at Tecnológico de Monterrey, one of Latin America’s most prestigious private universities, I had the opportunity to serve as a Teaching Assistant for three semesters, participating actively in both theoretical and practical instruction.

My responsibilities spanned co-teaching and developing lab sessions for the following advanced courses:

  • Signals & Systems
  • Control Systems
  • Lab of Automation

These classes combined rigorous theoretical foundations with hands-on laboratory work, ensuring students gained practical experience in applying their knowledge. Some of the key projects I helped design and supervise included:

  • Developing PLC-controlled motor systems for speed and torque control
  • Implementing automated door systems with real-world industrial logic
  • Simulating traffic light control systems to demonstrate logic sequencing

In my final semester as a TA, I had the privilege of participating in an industry-academia partnership with Generac, a global leader in energy solutions. This collaboration allowed us to guide students through real-life industrial automation challenges, integrating communication protocols and industrial devices into functional solutions aligned with company needs.

This experience significantly sharpened my ability to translate complex engineering concepts into accessible learning experiences, blending academic instruction with industry relevance.

Beginning of My Teaching Journey

My teaching experience began at the age of seventeen through a grassroots initiative in my hometown, Zapotiltic, Jalisco, Mexico. Motivated by the lack of academic resources in rural communities, I started a mathematics training program at Nezahualcóyotl Secondary School, where I had studied years before. The goal was to identify promising students from seventh to ninth grade and provide them with high-level math instruction aimed at preparing them for academic competitions at the regional and state level.

Program Structure and Methodology

The program was designed with structured phases:

  • Annual diagnostic evaluations to select the most talented students across all grades.
  • After-school sessions focused on mathematical problem-solving, logic, and critical thinking.
  • Mock exams and competitive drills to build resilience under pressure and familiarity with competition formats.
  • Focus on theory combined with interactive learning techniques to maintain engagement.

Impact and Results

The program had an immediate effect:

  • In its first year, the school earned its highest performance in state competitions, with six out of nine students earning medals.
  • Regular participation in multiple contests elevated the academic profile of the school and gave students access to broader opportunities.
  • Students developed not only academic skills but also soft skills like perseverance, confidence, and teamwork.

Expanding My Impact: Líderes del Mañana Scholarship

These early community efforts contributed to me earning the Líderes del Mañana scholarship from Tecnológico de Monterrey, Mexico's top private university. The program selects students based on academic excellence and community leadership, granting them a full scholarship to pursue higher education. This opportunity allowed me to expand my teaching projects and gain new skills in leadership and social impact.

Founding CreateMath

With access to university resources, I formalized my teaching into CreateMath, a student-led initiative focused on democratizing access to competitive math education. CreateMath became a multi-year, multi-location outreach project with a broad scope.

CreateMath founding members.

Achievements of CreateMath

  • Reached over 300 students from elementary, middle, and high schools.
  • Established collaborations with six different schools across Jalisco, customizing programs according to local needs.
  • Weekly training sessions supplemented by weekend workshops and intensive sessions before competitions.
  • Regular mock exams and leaderboard systems to encourage healthy competition and academic growth.
  • Personalized mentorship programs connecting university students with young learners.
CreateMath group picture, Jalisco's state math competition 2017.

Organizing Large-Scale Competitions

One of the highlights of CreateMath was the organization of two state-level mathematics competitions hosted at Tecnológico de Monterrey:

  • Each event welcomed around 200 students from diverse municipalities in Jalisco.
  • The competitions included in place evaluation with instructor from across the participating schools to provide transparency.
  • We created an in-house competitive environment, organizing a large-scale event bigger than any student organization before.

Skills Gained and Long-Term Impact

This experience helped me develop leadership, project management, and curriculum development skills. It reinforced my dedication to education as a tool for social transformation. Even after graduation, the methods and materials from CreateMath have continued to be used by other volunteers, leaving a lasting impact on hundreds of students.

Final Reflections

Across every stage of my teaching journey — from grassroots math coaching in local schools to advanced university instruction and prestigious international programs — I have developed a versatile and adaptive teaching style grounded in engagement, creativity, and real-world application.

These experiences have not only strengthened my technical teaching abilities but have also shaped my communication style, leadership approach, and capacity to design transformative educational experiences that reach diverse audiences in both in-person and remote environments.

One of the principles that defines my teaching philosophy is witnessing the long-term success of my students. In my young career, I have already had the privilege of seeing many of my former students go on to achieve their academic and professional goals — from graduating in a wide range of university programs to some now pursuing graduate and doctorate-level studies. Observing their progress is a continuous reminder of the positive ripple effect that education can have on individuals and communities.

Above all, teaching remains one of the most fulfilling and meaningful aspects of my professional life. I am always excited to seek out new challenges, create engaging learning environments, and continue contributing to the personal and academic growth of others.
